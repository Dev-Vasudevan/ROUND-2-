EC decision on Shiv ena: NCP chiefSharad Pawar ask' ally Uddhav to accept it,
take new symbol

PUNE:After the Election Commission of India (ECI) announced on Friday that the party name, "Shiv Sena", and symbol "bow and arrow" be given to Maharashtra chief minister Eknath Shinde's faction, Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) chief Sharad Pawar asked his ally Uddhav Thackeray, who had formed another faction, to accept the decision and take a new symbol.

He stated that the lossof the "bow and arrow" symbol would not ahev any major impact on them and that people would accept the new symbol.

"it's the decision of the Election Commission. Once a decision is taken, there can be no discussion. Accept it and take a new symbol. It(the loss of the old symbol) is not going to have any major impact as people will accept(the new symbol). It just would remain in the discussion for the next 15-30 days, that's it," Pawar said.
